Procedures & Services

We offer comprehensive dental services including: preventive care (cleanings, exams, X-rays), restorative dentistry (fillings, crowns, bridges), cosmetic dentistry (veneers, whitening, smile makeovers), dental implants, dentures, root canals, extractions, sedation dentistry, and emergency dental care.

Most people should have their teeth professionally cleaned every six months. However, some patients with gum disease or other oral health issues may need more frequent cleanings. We'll recommend a schedule based on your individual needs.

The implant procedure itself is performed under local anesthesia or sedation, so you won't feel pain during the procedure. Afterward, most patients report minimal discomfort that can be managed with over-the-counter pain medication. We also offer sedation options for a more comfortable experience.

With proper care, dental implants can last a lifetime. The titanium implant post fuses with your jawbone and is designed to be a permanent tooth replacement. The crown on top may need to be replaced every 10-15 years due to normal wear, but the implant itself should last indefinitely.

Sedation & Comfort

Yes! We offer multiple sedation options including I.V. sedation (deep sedation), oral sedation (pills), and nitrous oxide (laughing gas). Sedation dentistry is perfect for patients with dental anxiety, fear, or those undergoing lengthy procedures.

Yes, sedation dentistry is very safe when administered by trained professionals. We carefully monitor your vital signs throughout the procedure and adjust sedation levels as needed. We'll review your medical history to ensure sedation is appropriate for you.

We understand dental anxiety is common and take it seriously. We offer sedation options, take time to explain procedures, work at your pace, and create a calming environment. Many anxious patients find they can relax completely with our sedation dentistry options. Let us know about your concerns—we're here to help.

Emergency Care

Dental emergencies include: severe tooth pain, knocked-out teeth, broken or cracked teeth, lost fillings or crowns, abscesses or infections, severe bleeding, facial swelling, or trauma to the mouth or jaw. If you're experiencing any of these, call us immediately.

Yes! We prioritize dental emergencies and will make every effort to see you the same day. Call our office immediately if you have a dental emergency, even if you're not currently a patient. We're here to help when you need us most.
